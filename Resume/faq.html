What is a CV?

A CV, short for 'curriculum vitae', is a comprehensive document that outlines your lifeâ€™s journey in terms of education, work experience, and skills. It provides potential employers with a detailed snapshot of your professional background, helping them assess your fit for a job and decide whether to invite you for an interview.

What should I include in my curriculum vitae?

Your CV should be tailored to each job application, highlighting the most relevant details. At a minimum, your CV should include:

  • Personal and contact details
  • Education and qualifications
  • Work experience
  • Skills and competencies
  • Key attributes and qualities

What is a chronological CV, and what order should it be in?

A chronological CV lists your experiences in reverse-chronological order, starting with the most recent. This format is widely used and helps employers quickly see your most recent roles and achievements. The recommended structure for a chronological CV is:

  • Personal and contact details
  • A concise personal profile
  • Education and qualifications
  • Work experience
  • Languages
  • Skills and competencies
  • Personal attributes and interests

How to create your own CV (or application letter)

You can create a professional CV using word processing software like Microsoft Word and then convert it to PDF format. Once your CV and application letter are ready, you can send them along with a tailored email to apply for your desired job.

Tips for creating your perfect CV

To make your CV stand out, follow these general tips:

  • Include only relevant information that adds value to your application and is of interest to potential employers.
  • Avoid mentioning hobbies or interests that could lead to awkward questions.
  • Place the most important information on the first page, including a brief personal profile.
  • Use bullet points and numbered lists to enhance readability.
  • Stick to the chronological CV structure unless the job listing specifies otherwise.
